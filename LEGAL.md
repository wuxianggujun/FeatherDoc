# Legal Notes

This file is a short repository guide for the current FeatherDoc licensing
layout. It is not a substitute for the actual license texts.

## What Controls This Repository

- Formal terms for fork-specific FeatherDoc modifications: `LICENSE`
- Preserved upstream DuckX MIT text: `LICENSE.upstream-mit`
- Distribution notice summary: `NOTICE`
- Chinese practical guide: `docs/licensing_zh.rst`

## Current Position

This fork should be described as `source-available`, not as a pure MIT open
source project.

- Fork-specific modifications are released under non-commercial,
  source-available terms.
- Upstream DuckX-derived portions still retain their preserved MIT notice.
- Bundled third-party dependencies keep their own original licenses.

## Practical Summary

You can generally:

- Read and study the source code.
- Modify the fork for personal, educational, research, or other
  non-commercial purposes.
- Redistribute non-commercial copies while preserving required notices.

You may not, without separate written permission from the copyright holder:

- Use the fork-specific modifications for commercial purposes.
- Sell the repository source code.
- Sell modified source code.
- Bundle the fork-specific modifications into paid products, paid services,
  SaaS, commercial SDKs, OEM offerings, or customer-facing commercial delivery.

## Important Boundary

Changing this fork's `LICENSE` does not cancel upstream MIT rights that already
exist in the DuckX-derived portions, and it does not replace the original
licenses of bundled third-party dependencies.

## Recommendation

When referencing this repository publicly, use wording close to:

`FeatherDoc fork modifications are distributed under a non-commercial
source-available license; upstream DuckX-derived portions preserve their MIT
notice; bundled third-party dependencies remain under their own licenses.`

## Legal Caution

If you need commercial use, obtain separate written authorization first.

If you need legal certainty for external distribution or business use, ask a
qualified lawyer to review the current license structure.
